OBSERVATIONS - Question 1: Linux Environment Verification

1. whoami / id / groups
   These commands revealed my username (bikash) and confirmed the groups
   my account belongs to. This is useful to verify what permission level
   I have before accessing company resources.

2. echo $SHELL
   This showed my default shell is /bin/bash, meaning bash interprets
   all the commands I type.

3. pwd
   This confirmed my current working directory, letting me verify
   exactly where in the filesystem I was operating before running
   further commands.

4. ls -la
   This listed all files and folders (including hidden ones) in my
   workspace along with permissions and ownership, giving me a full
   view of what exists in the current directory.

5. ping -c 4 google.com
   This sent 4 packets to google.com and received replies with 0%
   packet loss, confirming my Linux environment has working network
   connectivity to the internet.
